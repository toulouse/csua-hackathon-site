Spring 2012 Hackathon

Join us for a night full of food, fun, and furious coding!

Welcome Come out and code something cool.

Welcome to our 18 hour coding marathon! We at the CSUA want you to write that hack you've always wanted, or really want right now. Hack with us! We'll feed you, bring in some kickass judges, and award some awesome prizes.

Sponsors Awesomeness brought to you by…

Ness

Ness Computing’s mission is to make search personal. By combining its understanding of human nature with its expertise in search, recommendations and social networking, the company can deliver experiences that are more deeply personal than ever before.

The company’s first offering is Ness, a personal search engine that builds a digital likeness that reflects each person’s unique tastes, preferences and personality. Ness combines social graph data mining and machine learning to bring personal relevance to the field of search.

Ness Computing’s team has expertise in information retrieval, applied machine learning, natural language processing, collaborative filtering, and user interface engineering, and has built successful products and technologies at Apple, Google, Ning, Oracle, Palantir, and Yahoo.

Heroku

Heroku (pronounced her-OH-koo) is a cloud application platform – a new way of building and deploying web apps. Its service lets app developers spend 100% of their time on their application code, not managing servers, deployment, ongoing operations, or scaling.

Heroku's platform architecture includes tools for deployment and management, a runtime for scalability, fault tolerance, and an add-ons system for extending the capabilities of its platform.

The San Francisco, CA-based company is a passionate and active supporter of Ruby technologies and cloud architecture. Tens of thousands of developers worldwide have deployed over 100,000+ applications to Heroku. For more information, please visit http://heroku.com and http://blog.heroku.com or follow Heroku on Twitter at http://twitter.com/heroku.

Submission Guidelines How to participate.

  • Registration for CSUA Hackathon is day-of. We will provide your team with a private GitHub repository, so please come prepared with your GitHub account already set up!
  • PLEASE NOTE: We are requiring GitHub submissions this year. Submissions are not required to be either open or closed, but a uniform location for projects will aid the judges in making fair evaluations of the projects.
    • GitHub has excellent tutorials on how to use the version control system Git and how to setup a repository on Github. Source control is a critical piece of the software development workflow.
    • For those who strongly prefer SVN, GitHub does offer support for SVN clients.
    • The CSUA is offering a GitHub helpsession on Wednesday from 6PM - 8PM in 380 Soda.
    • Your team will retain all rights to the code you submit. The CSUA makes no claim to ownership or copyright over any of your work.
  • During your final submission, it is very important to write a clear, concise description of your project. "We coded a cool project using Kinect," may be interesting, but will not help the judges identify and recall your project during the judging process.
  • Further details will be released on the day of the event.

Rules This school's got 'em.

Teams

  • Teams of any size or composition are welcome to participate and present!
  • Only teams of composed entirely of UC Berkeley undergraduate students are eligible to win prizes.
  • Prizes will be awarded according to team size up to a maximum of four. This means that teams with greater than four members may be eligible, but will need to distribute the prizes amongst themselves.

Content

  • We want everyone to hack on something great and hope that you come to scratch your own itches.
  • Class projects may be disqualified.
    • The hackathon is about exploring your awesomeness, independent of what someone else is telling you to do. So be awesome! Learn a new language, do something you haven't done before! Go out on a limb!
